2401

chata'ah

feminine of 'chet'' (2399); an offence, or a sacrifice for it:--sin (offering).

(Aramaic) corresponding to 'chata'ah' (2401); an offence, and the penalty or sacrifice for it:--sin (offering).

chatta'ah
khat-taw-aw'

or chattacth {khat-tawth'}; from 'chata'' (2398); an offence (sometimes habitual sinfulness), and its penalty, occasion, sacrifice, or expiation; also (concretely) an offender:--punishment (of sin), purifying(-fication for sin), sin(-ner, offering).

2416

chay
khah'-ee

from 'chayah' (2421); alive; hence, raw (flesh); fresh (plant, water, year), strong; also (as noun, especially in the feminine singular and masculine plural) life (or living thing), whether literally or figuratively:--+ age, alive, appetite, (wild) beast, company, congregation, life(-time), live(-ly), living (creature, thing), maintenance, + merry, multitude, + (be) old, quick, raw, running, springing, troop.

2418
